⬡ What is Leibniz Layer™

A new layer
in the AI stack

Leibniz Layer™ is a cryptographic verification protocol for AI agent decisions. Named after Gottfried Wilhelm Leibniz — mathematician, logician, and pioneer of deterministic reasoning — it brings the same rigour to modern AI systems.

Just as TLS secured the web without changing HTTP, Leibniz Layer secures AI agent behaviour without changing your existing stack. It sits between your agents and the world, sealing every decision into a tamper-evident chain.

DCL Evaluator is the first product implementing this layer — available today.

⬡ Core properties

01
Deterministic

Identical input + policy = identical decision. 100% reproducible across runs, machines, and time. Unlike probabilistic LLM filters, Leibniz Layer never surprises you.

02
Tamper-evident

Every agent action is sealed with SHA-256 into a Merkle hash chain. Modify any past record — the entire chain invalidates. Mathematical proof of integrity.

03
Auditable

Full audit trail exportable as tamper-evident PDF, JSON, or CSV. Ready for regulators, insurers, and legal proceedings. Every decision leaves a permanent record.

04
Model-agnostic

Works with Claude, GPT-4, Grok, Gemini, DeepSeek, and local Ollama models. The verification layer is independent of which LLM powers your agent.

05
Offline-capable

Run fully air-gapped with local models. Zero data leaves your infrastructure. Critical for regulated industries: finance, healthcare, defence, government.

06
Compliance-ready

Built-in policy templates for EU AI Act, GDPR, Finance (AML), Medical (HIPAA), and Red Team scenarios. Leibniz Layer speaks the language of regulators.
